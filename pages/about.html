// What this is

The Humanist Engineer is my personal learning hub and engineering portfolio. It tracks my path from CS at OSU Tulsa toward ECE graduate work at CU Boulder, with systems programming, computer architecture, embedded work, Linux customization, technical reading, and long-form notes all in one place.

The original C++ learning journal still matters here: those articles and projects are preserved as a systems track. The broader site is now the operating notebook around that work: what I am building, what I am reading, what I am studying, and how the pieces connect.

Code and notes are written in my own words as I learn. AI can help with site structure and presentation, but it does not replace the underlying exercises, project work, or technical understanding.
